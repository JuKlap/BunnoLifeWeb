The domestic rabbit — more commonly known as a pet rabbit, bunny or bun — is the domesticated form of the European rabbit, a member of the lagomorph family.
Beginning in the 1980s, the idea of the domestic rabbit as a house companion, a so-called house rabbit similar to a house cat, was promoted. Rabbits can be litter box-trained and taught to come when called, but they require exercise and can damage a house that has not been "rabbit proofed" based on their innate need to chew.
Unwanted pet rabbits end up in animal shelters, especially after the Easter season. Pet rabbits, being a domesticated breed that lack survival instincts, do not fare well in the wild if they are abandoned or escape from captivity.
We are passionate about bunnies and here we are building a community where bun-parents can find all needed information of how best to take care of their bunnies.
We also take care of bunnies that lost their homes / were abandoned and help them find a new family who would love them.
